Dyson Swarm Calculator

Python Tool · Type II Civilizational Engineering · 2026

Introduction

A physics-based simulator for Type II Kardashev civilizational engineering. This engine models the physical and logistical requirements for constructing a Dyson Swarm — a megastructure of solar collectors — around a G-type star like Sol (G2V). It turns astrophysical concepts into readable engineering data: construction timelines, planetary consumption, and orbital parameters.

Core Physics

  • Thermal Equilibrium (Goldilocks Radius) — Using the Stefan-Boltzmann Law, the distance where a satellite maintains a specific operating temperature is calculated, preventing the swarm from melting or freezing.
  • Orbital Dynamics — Using Kepler's Third Law, the engine determines orbital period and required Δv to reach the swarm's destination from a 1 AU starting point.
  • Statite Limit (Radiation Pressure) — Calculates the Area-to-Mass ratio to determine whether a satellite is gravity-dominated or a light sail dominated by radiation pressure.
  • Exponential Growth via Sigmoid Functions — Models self-replicating construction robots with positive feedback loops, accounting for energy from active satellites accelerating production of new ones.

Expert Contacts

Jonas Seiler — ESA, Advanced Concepts Team
Explained that 10¹⁴ satellites per year is a high and optimistic number given SpaceX launches ~2 rockets per week optimistically. Advised that production capacity growth would likely follow a sigmoid function.
Jeanette Heiligers — TU Delft, Faculty of Aerospace Engineering
Provided a better methodology for the project. Explained why attitude-control was easier and quicker, why active station-keeping would always be a necessity, and that real-world results will always deviate from the theoretical model.
