FLL — First Lego League 24/25

Innovation Project · Assisted by Daniël. Kuijvenhoven · 2024–2025

Overview

A Lego League innovation idea: a 40 kg Earth observation satellite designed to follow ESA orbital guidelines and be mathematically sound from launch to deorbit.

1. Rocket Fuel

Normal fuel was too weak and too heavy, forcing excessive resource expenditure on the launch vehicle. Hydrolox was chosen instead — it provides enough Δv for the mission and any improvisations during the launch and orbiting sequence.

2. Mission

Earth observation. With a multi-spectral camera, the satellite can scan 15,000–25,000 m² with a resolution of 5–10 m².

3. Orbit

An orbit of 250 km was selected. Analysis showed that the original 500 km orbit would keep the satellite in space longer than ESA guidelines allow, classifying it as space debris. At 250 km, atmospheric drag causes natural deorbit within 5 years.

4. Mass

Standard materials were too heavy for the 1,200 kN launch vehicle. Carbon fiber was selected as a lighter and cheaper alternative, bringing the launch vehicle within acceptable mass margins.
