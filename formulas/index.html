Average velocity v = d / t Displacement per unit time for uniform motion.

Rotational Motion

Name Formula Description
Angular velocity ω = Δθ / Δt Rate of change of angular position.
Angular acceleration α = Δω / Δt Rate of change of angular velocity.
Tangential/centripetal relation v = ωr Linear speed at radius r for a rotating body.
Centripetal acceleration a_c = v²/r = ω²r Center-seeking acceleration for circular motion.
Centripetal force F_c = mv²/r Net inward force required for circular motion.
Torque τ = rF·sinθ = Iα Rotational analogue of force.
Moment of inertia (point mass) I = mr² Rotational inertia of a point mass about an axis.
Moment of inertia (solid disk/cylinder) I = ½mr² Rotational inertia of a uniform solid disk about its central axis.
Moment of inertia (solid sphere) I = (2/5)mr² Rotational inertia of a uniform solid sphere about a diameter.
Moment of inertia (thin rod, center) I = (1/12)mL² Rotational inertia of a rod about its center, perpendicular to its length.
Angular momentum L = Iω Energy stored due to height in a uniform gravitational field.
Conservation of angular momentum I₁ω₁ = I₂ω₂ Angular momentum is conserved absent external torque.
Rotational kinetic energy KE_rot = ½Iω² Kinetic energy due to rotation.
Parallel axis theorem I = I_cm + md² Moment of inertia about an axis parallel to one through the center of mass.
